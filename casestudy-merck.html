Merck Millipore is an international company specializing in the sale of life science manufacturing equipment. They needed a Sales Application that would streamline the purchasing process for their employees and consumers. I helped bring this application to life by designing a series of illustrations displaying biomanufacturing processes and products.

Process and Wireframes

The process initiated with close collaboration with Milliporeâ€™s science teams. Rough outlines of the processes to be illustrated were delivered by Millipore and then converted into blueprints displaying the products, their connections, their names, and general scale and appearance.

With these outlines, the experts at Millipore were able to verify the accuracy of the processes and our team at Maark was able to further familiarize ourselves with the product and utilize a jumping-off point for more complex illustrations.

The Wireframe Workflow:

Visual Design

I collaborated with my team members at Maark to create a system of visual design for the project. The choice was made to display the products in the form of illustrations to highlight the products. The illustrations were initially black and white, but the move was eventually made to transition them into color to highlight the individual products and add vibrancy to the individual elements.

Illustration process:

Technical Design

The scope of the illustrations was initially very small, thus the illustrations were static. Continuing success with the project prompted Millipore to expand the scope of the program to encompass even more products and processes, which resulted in additional challenges.

Problem Solution
Many hundreds of processes would need to be displayed under the new project scope. A modular system of illustration was adopted to allow for interchangeable steps within processes.
Large file sizes Modular steps were separated into individual files, by process type and stage. These could easily be combined, edited and labeled in less resource-intensive photoshop files.
Organization The previous naming scheme was adapted to accommodate the additional graphics. The now defunct plugin slicy was used to easily export photoshop artboards quickly, at consistent names and sizes.
